INTRODUCTION

1.1 Background of the Study

The rapid advancement of Information and Communication Technology

(ICT) has significantly transformed the way organizations manage data,

communication, and administrative processes. In the educational sector,

ICT has become a critical tool for improving efficiency, accuracy, and

decision-making. Departments within higher institutions are responsible

for managing essential data such as student records, staff information,

and financial transactions. However, despite the availability of modern

technologies, many departments still depend on manual or semi-

automated systems, which are often inefficient, time-consuming, and

prone to errors.

Student record management is one of the most sensitive and data-

intensive operations in any academic department. It involves handling

personal

information, academic performance, enrollment history, and

other critical data. Manual handling of such records can lead to duplication,

data inconsistency, and difficulty in retrieving historical information when

needed. According to Shelly and Rosenblatt (2018), traditional file-based

systems lack the flexibility and reliability required for managing large

datasets, thereby increasing the risk of data loss and inefficiency.

Similarly, staff record management plays a vital role in ensuring smooth

departmental operations. Departments must keep track of

staff

qualifications, roles, teaching responsibilities, and service history. When

these records are maintained manually, it becomes difficult to update

information, monitor staff performance, and assign responsibilities

effectively. Pressman and Maxim (2020) note that poorly managed

information systems can hinder organizational productivity and lead to

poor resource utilization.

In addition to student and staff

records,

financial management—

particularly expense tracking—is a crucial aspect of departmental

administration. Departments regularly incur expenses related to academic

activities, maintenance,

logistics, and other operational needs. When

expense records are not properly managed, it can result in financial

mismanagement, lack of transparency, and difficulty in auditing. Romney

and Steinbart (2021) emphasize that an effective accounting information

system is essential

for ensuring accountability, accuracy, and proper

financial reporting in organizations.

The limitations of manual systems, including slow processing speed, lack

of real-time access, poor data security, and high susceptibility to human

error, highlight the need for computerized solutions. Laudon and Laudon

(2020) argue that digital

information systems enhance organizational

performance by enabling faster data processing, improved data accuracy,

and better decision-making capabilities. Likewise, O’Brien and Marakas

(2019) state that integrated information systems provide a centralized

platform that supports data sharing, communication, and operational

efficiency.

A web-based Departmental Management System (DMS) offers a

comprehensive solution to these challenges by integrating student

management, staff administration, and expense tracking into a single

platform. Such a system allows authorized users to access, update, and

manage information in real time, regardless of location. It also improves

data security through authentication and access control mechanisms,

while ensuring that records are properly stored and backed up.

Furthermore, the adoption of web-based systems aligns with the global

trend toward digital transformation in education, where institutions are

increasingly leveraging technology to enhance administrative and

academic processes. By automating routine tasks and providing accurate

and timely information, a Departmental Management System can

significantly

improve

productivity,

accountability,

and

overall

departmental performance.

Therefore, this study focuses on the design and implementation of a web-

based Departmental Management System specifically tailored to manage

student records, staff information, and departmental expenses efficiently

and effectively.

1.2 Statement of the Problem

Many departments face significant challenges in managing student, staff,

and financial

records due to reliance on manual systems. These

challenges include:

i. Difficulty in storing and retrieving student and staff information

ii. Loss or misplacement of academic and administrative records

iii. Inefficient tracking of staff roles and responsibilities

iv. Poor documentation and monitoring of departmental expenses

v. Lack of transparency and accountability in financial records

vi. High risk of errors and data inconsistencies

vii. Time-consuming administrative processes

These problems reduce productivity, affect decision-making, and may

lead to financial mismanagement. Therefore, there is a need for a

computerized system that can efficiently manage these core departmental

functions.

1.3 Aim and Objectives of the Study

The main aim of this project is to design and implement a Departmental

Management System focused on managing student, staff, and expense

records.

The specific objectives are to:
